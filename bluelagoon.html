The Blue Lagoon & Comino Island

📍 Comino Island, Malta

The Blue Lagoon is a paradise pool nestled between Comino and the islet of Cominotto. Historically, Comino island served as a hiding spot for pirates and smugglers during the Middle Ages due to its secluded bays and complex cave systems. Today, its white sandy seafloor reflects the sunlight to create a glowing cyan surface, making it one of the top locations in Europe for swimming and snorkeling.

Crystal-clear currents ideal for boat tours and anchoring
Scenic coastal cliffs bordering the turquoise lagoon

The Majestic Blue Grotto

📍 Zurrieq, Southern Coast of Malta

Located on the southern cliffs of Malta, the Blue Grotto is a complex system of seven interconnected sea caves, highlighted by a massive 30-meter main stone arch. Deeply rooted in local history, mythology associates these caverns with the home of sea sirens who enchanted sailors. The location is globally famous for its morning phenomenon where light reflects a phosphorescent blue glow onto the cave walls.
